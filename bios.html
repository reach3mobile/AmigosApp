Mike Cerletti
1939-2012
(In Memorium)

Beloved by all, former Amigo Mike Cerletti passed earlier this year at the age of 72. Mike loved New Mexico and especially the opportunity to travel with the Amigos on their annual goodwill trip. Mike was New Mexico's first tourism secretary and served as such under two governors, King and Richardson and was named State Tourism Director of the year in 2010 by the Council of State Tourism Directors. Mike, who was a native of San Francisco, had a Bachelor of Science degree from San Jose State University and moved to Santa Fe with his wife, Helen, in 1974. He became a managing partner and co-developer of several hotels including the St. Francis, La Posada, and the Plaza Real and was President of the Southwest Hospitality Group and managing partner of the former Rancho Encantado. Mike was given credit for creating an ecotourism program that helped increase regional advertising, and in addition, the state's tourism industry grew from $4.2 billion annually in 2005 to $5.7 billion by 2009. "Mike was the best promoter ever of New Mexico as a destination" said a former colleague. "His enthusiasm and his passion for the Land of Enchantment was contagious as he represented the state throughout the country and the world."

– Las Cruces

Dr. Janet Green is to be commended for envisioning and leading The New Mexico Amigos on their 2012 Goodwill Ambassadorial Trip May 13-20 to first, our nation's Capitol, Washington, DC as well as to Annapolis, Maryland, Atlanta, Georgia, and San Antonio, Texas. Dr. Green holds both a Master of Science degree from the College of Agriculture and Home Economics in Family and Consumer Sciences specializing in Tourism Studies and a doctorate, Ed.D., from the College of Education, both from New Mexico State University. She was the project director of the Rural Economic Development Through Tourism (REDTT) Cooperative Extension Project from 1993-1995 at New Mexico State University and has served as a political appointee for three gubernatorial terms, the most recent as the two-term Cabinet Secretary of the New Mexico Department of Tourism under Governor Gary Johnson.

SAM PICK

Next

Sam Pick
Former Mayor, Santa Fe

Sam Pick, the past and, to many, perpetual Mayor of Santa Fe, embodies the Amigo spirit like no other. He's flamboyant, out-spoken, sometimes controversial, but undeniably the major force behind what made Santa Fe of the past what it is today. Santa Fe's Mayor from 1975-1977 and 1986-1992, the quick-witted, sharp-tongued, ever-loving Sam led the transformation of the oldest capital city in the US from a quaint, off the beaten path tourist town to one of the world's preeminent cultural Meccas and prime vacation destinations. Señor Sam is all about style, and Santa Fe Style became a household name as well as vaulting the City Different to the top of the Conde Nast Most Desirable Cities list as a result. Here's to Sam and his most often exclaimed proclamation – Who Loves You!
